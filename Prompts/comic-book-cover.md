# Comic Book Cover Prompt

Transform the attached photograph into a **1940s Golden Age comic book cover** illustration, in the style of early *Superman* or *Captain America* covers.

## Art Style

- Convert the photo into hand-drawn cel-style art: bold, confident black ink outlines and large unbroken fields of solid flat color.
- Use a restricted four-color-press palette — roughly 5 to 7 ink colors total (red, blue, yellow, flesh tone, black) on one or two flat background color zones.
- **No** cross-hatching, rendered gradients, fine line texture, airbrush, photorealism, or Ben-Day dot halftones.
- Shadows are single flat black shapes, not hatched.

## Focus & Detail

- Spend detail **only** on (1) the child or children and (2) the invention they are working on. Everything else simplifies to flat color and bold outline.
- The background is one or two flat color zones, not a detailed room — no clutter, no extra props.
- Stylize faces and figures enough that they read as illustrated comic characters rather than identifiable people — simplified inked features.
- Preserve the general action and the device from the photo so the scene still reads as kids building something real.

## Text Rules

- Render **only** the text specified below — exact wording, nothing else.
- Do **not** invent any other posters, signs, slogans, labels, or lettering. Background surfaces are blank.

## Cover Layout

Compose as a full vintage newsstand comic cover with classic cover furniture:

- **Masthead:** Faithfully replicate the attached masthead banner across the top, three stacked lines:
  - "THE LEAGUE OF" (top, smaller, yellow with black outline)
  - "AMAZING" (center, largest, orange `#F47920` with black outline)
  - "PROGRAMMERS" (bottom, medium, yellow with black outline)

  Bold hand-drawn vintage comic logo type with heavy black drop shadow.
- **Lightning bolts:** Flanking "THE LEAGUE OF", the attached lightning-bolt icon — a flat solid orange (`#F47920`) bolt, clean flat shape, no added detail — one upright bolt to the left and one to the right, same size, in place of stars.
- **Subhead strip:** Directly under the logo, reading "SMART MINDS · BOLD IDEAS · BRIGHT FUTURES".
- **Price corner box** (top left): "10¢".
- **Issue/date corner** (top right): "NO. 1 / JUNE".
- **Approval badge** (upper right): a circular stamp using the attached robot logo as its centerpiece — a gray-and-orange robot bust with an orange chest bolt inside a heavy black ring, kept faithful to the attached image as a flat-color emblem, not re-illustrated. Curve "APPROVED BY" along the inside top of the ring and "THE CREATORS CODE AUTHORITY" along the inside bottom, small bold black caps following the circle.
- **Callout box:** One small rectangular callout along the left edge reading "Whose Robot Will Prevail?", placed to the left of the robots so it does not cover any character's face and does not obscure any robot. Move the robots a bit to the right so the callout does not obscure them.
- **Cover title:** A large dramatic hand-lettered title in a jagged starburst banner, lower third: "ROBOT RIOT".
- **Robots:** Ensure robots have low detail like the characters, and have physically plausible front grippers, pushers, hooks, or arms.
- **Background:** Use a yellow background behind the characters, below the subheading.

## Mood

Heroic, optimistic, aspirational, exciting, competitive — kids who are proud to be battling with robots they created.
